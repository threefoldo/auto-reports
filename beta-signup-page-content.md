<a name="design"></a>
## Design Recommendations

### Visual Style

**Tone**: Academic, professional, trustworthy - not flashy startup

**Colors**:
- Primary: Deep blue (academic, trustworthy)
- Secondary: Warm orange or green (optimistic, growth)
- Accent: Light gray for backgrounds
- Avoid: Bright reds (stress), all black (too formal)

**Typography**:
- Headlines: Clean sans-serif (Inter, Poppins, or Montserrat)
- Body: Highly readable (Georgia, Merriweather, or Source Sans Pro)
- Size: Large enough for stressed PhD students (16px minimum body)

**Imagery**:
- Illustrations over photos (more approachable)
- Diverse representation
- Academic setting cues (books, libraries, laptops)
- Avoid: Stock photos of fake "students" in labs

---

### Layout Structure

**Above the fold**:
- Clear headline
- Subheadline explaining benefit
- Large CTA button
- Minimal navigation (don't distract)

**Scroll progression**:
1. Problem (empathy)
2. Solution (hope)
3. How it works (clarity)
4. Beta details (action)
5. Social proof (trust)
6. FAQ (objection handling)
7. Final CTA (conversion)

**Mobile-first**:
- 70%+ traffic will be mobile
- Thumb-friendly CTA buttons
- Readable on small screens
- Fast loading

---

### Trust Signals

**Include**:
- ‚úì Your academic credentials (PhD student at [university])
- ‚úì Research methodology transparency
- ‚úì Privacy policy link
- ‚úì "No credit card required"
- ‚úì "Unsubscribe anytime"
- ‚úì Social proof (waitlist count, testimonials)

**Avoid**:
- ‚úó Fake urgency ("Only 3 spots left!")
- ‚úó Over-promises ("Guarantee graduation in 6 months!")
- ‚úó Corporate stock photos
- ‚úó Too much marketing speak

---

### CTA Button Best Practices

**Text Options** (A/B test these):
- "Join Beta Program" (clear, specific)
- "Get Better Feedback" (benefit-driven)
- "I'm Ready" (commitment)

**Design**:
- Large (min 44px height for mobile tapping)
- Contrasting color
- Plenty of white space around it
- Above and below fold
- Repeat 3-4 times on page

## Final Notes

**Remember**:
- Clear > Clever (straightforward copy converts better)
- Benefits > Features (they care about outcomes, not functions)
- Specific > Vague (concrete examples build trust)
- Simple > Complex (fewer fields = higher conversions)

**Most Important**:
Your landing page should answer three questions immediately:
1. What is this?
2. How does it help me?
3. What do I need to do?

If a PhD student lands on your page and can't answer all three in 10 seconds,
simplify.
