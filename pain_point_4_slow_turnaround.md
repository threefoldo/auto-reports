# Pain Point #4: Slow Turnaround Times

## Overview

**The Problem**: Advisors take weeks or months to return feedback on dissertation chapters, causing delays in graduation, increased costs, and high anxiety.

**Severity**: **MEDIUM-HIGH** - Financial and timeline impact, plus psychological stress

**Prevalence**: One of the most common complaints across all graduate student communities

**Typical Experience**: Students wait 6-12 weeks (or longer) for feedback that should take 3-4 weeks

---

## The Problem in Detail

### What Students Experience

**Common Timeline**:
- Student submits chapter
- Week 1: No response
- Week 2: Email: "Did you receive my chapter?"
- Week 3: Advisor: "Yes, I'll get to it soon"
- Week 4-8: Silence
- Week 9: Follow-up email
- Week 10-12: Finally receive feedback (maybe)

**Some students report**:
- **3 weeks** considered "fast" (when 1 week should be achievable)
- **6-12 weeks** common
- **6 months** for some unlucky students
- **Never** in extreme cases

### What the Research Says

**Reasonable Timeframes**:
> "The consensus from experienced dissertation advisors is that **3-4 weeks is an ample amount of time** for receiving feedback."

**Actual Practice Varies**:
- Fast turnaround: 1 week maximum (rare)
- Standard: 3 weeks (should be normal, often isn't)
- Acceptable range: 2 weeks to 1 month
- **Reality for many**: 6 months (!!)

### Real Reports from Students

**From Forums**:
> "My advisor takes **three weeks** to provide feedback"

> "I've been waiting **6 months** for chapters to be returned"

> "I submitted my chapter **spring break** and it's now **fall semester** - still no feedback"

**Best Practice (Often Not Followed)**:
> "Ideally, your supervisor will contact you within **a few days** to let you know they've received your draft and provide a timeline for when feedback will be ready."

```
Month 1: Submit Chapter 1
```

**Defense Timing**:
- Many universities have specific defense windows

**Job Market Timing**:
- Academic jobs: Hiring on annual cycle, miss year = wait another year
- Industry jobs: Offers expire, opportunities lost

**Funding Expiration**:

**Anxiety & Stress**:

**Quote from Student**:

## What Good Feedback Timing Looks Like

### Recommended Timeframes

**For Different Submission Types**:

| Submission Type | Reasonable Turnaround |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **Short piece (<10 pages)** | 3-7 days |
| **Chapter (~30-50 pages)** | 3-4 weeks |
| **Multiple chapters** | 4-6 weeks |
| **Full dissertation draft** | 6-8 weeks |
| **Brief questions via email** | 48-72 hours |
| **Urgent pre-defense edits** | 1 week |

**Communication Timeline**:
- **Within 2-3 days**: Acknowledge receipt
- **Within 1 week**: Provide timeline for feedback ("I'll have comments by Oct 15")
- **On promised date** (or before): Deliver feedback
- **If delayed**: Proactively communicate new timeline

### Best Practices from Good Advisors

**Example of Excellent Practice**:
> "I aim to return chapter feedback within 2 weeks. I'll confirm receipt within 2 days and let you know my timeline. If something comes up that delays me, I'll email you immediately with a new expected date."

**Setting Mutual Deadlines**:
> "Students should submit a draft, **request comments within three weeks**, and schedule a meeting to receive both written and verbal input."

**Benefits**:
- Creates collaborative deadline
- Shows professional mutual respect
- Accountability for both parties
- Student can plan next steps

### Institutional Requirements (Minimum Standards)

**Princeton University**:
> PhD students must meet with dissertation advisor "on a frequency determined by their program, but **in no case less than once per semester**"

**Note**: This is MINIMUM. Best practice is much more frequent:
- Weekly or bi-weekly during active writing
- Monthly at minimum for all stages

**The Gap**: Institutions set minimums but don't enforce feedback turnaround times

## Practical Solutions

### For Students: Getting Faster Feedback

#### Strategy #1: Set Expectations Upfront

**Use Student-Advisor Feedback Agreement**:

```
FEEDBACK TIMELINE AGREEMENT

For chapter-length submissions (~30-50 pages):
- Acknowledgment of receipt: within __3__ days
- Feedback delivery: within __3-4__ weeks
- Meeting to discuss: within __1 week__ of feedback

For shorter submissions (<10 pages):
- Feedback delivery: within __1 week__

For urgent pre-defense edits:
- Fast-track timeline: within __1 week__

If timeline cannot be met:
- Advisor will notify student __immediately__
- Provide new expected date
- Explain reason for delay

Student agrees to:
- Submit polished, proofread drafts
- Not submit new work until feedback received on previous
- Be flexible if genuine emergencies arise

Signed:
Student: _____________ Date: _____
Advisor: _____________ Date: _____
```

**When to Create This**: First month of dissertation work

**Benefits**:
- Clear expectations both parties agree to
- Easier to follow up if agreement not kept
- Shows professionalism
- Can reference in follow-up emails

#### Strategy #2: Create Collaborative Deadlines

**When Submitting**:

**Email Template**:
```
Subject: Chapter 3 Submission - Feedback by [Date]?

Dear Professor [Name],

I'm submitting Chapter 3 (Methodology) for your review. The chapter is
45 pages and addresses [brief description].

Based on our previous discussions, would you be able to provide feedback
by [date 3-4 weeks out]? This would allow me to incorporate your
suggestions and submit Chapter 4 by [date], keeping me on track to
defend in [semester].

I'm also happy to schedule a meeting for [date after feedback deadline]
to discuss your comments in person.

Please let me know if this timeline works for you, or if you need more
time.

Thank you,
[Your name]

Attached: Chapter 3 - Methodology (45 pages)
```

**Key Elements**:
- âœ… Specific timeline request
- âœ… Reasoning (staying on track)
- âœ… Pre-scheduled meeting
- âœ… Flexibility ("or if you need more time")
- âœ… Clear subject line

**Psychology**: Much easier to ignore vague "please review" than specific timeline request

#### Strategy #3: Strategic Follow-Up Protocol

**Timeline**:

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Day 3** | Email to confirm receipt (if no acknowledgment) |
| **Week 3** | Polite check-in about timeline |
| **Week 5** | More direct follow-up |
| **Week 6** | Mention timeline concerns, reference agreement |
| **Week 7** | CC DGS or request meeting |
| **Week 8+** | Escalate to department leadership |

**Week 3 Follow-Up Template**:
```
Subject: Following up: Chapter 3 Submitted [Date]

Dear Professor [Name],

I wanted to follow up on Chapter 3, which I submitted on [date] three
weeks ago. I know you're very busy, and I wanted to check if you've had
a chance to review it.

Would you be able to provide a timeline for when you expect to have
feedback? This would help me plan my next steps.

Thank you,
[Your name]
```

**Week 5 Follow-Up Template** (More Direct):
```
Subject: Chapter 3 Feedback - Timeline Update?

Dear Professor [Name],

I'm following up again on Chapter 3 (submitted [date], 5 weeks ago).
I'm eager to incorporate your feedback and move forward.

I'm concerned about my timeline for [defense semester]. To stay on
schedule, I need to submit Chapter 4 by [date], which requires your
feedback on Chapter 3 first.

Could we schedule a brief meeting this week to discuss? Even if you
haven't had time for detailed written comments, your verbal feedback
would help me keep making progress.

I'm available [times].

Thank you,
[Your name]
```

**Week 7 Escalation**:
```
Subject: Assistance Needed - Chapter 3 Feedback Timeline

Dear Professor [Name],

CC: [Director of Graduate Studies]

I'm writing to seek guidance on moving forward with my dissertation.

I submitted Chapter 3 on [date] (7 weeks ago). Despite follow-up emails
on [dates], I have not yet received feedback or a timeline for when to
expect it.

I'm concerned because:
- I cannot move forward with Chapter 4 without feedback on Chapter 3
- My defense timeline for [semester] is at risk
- My funding ends in [date]

Professor [DGS name], could you advise on how to proceed? I want to be
respectful of Professor [Advisor]'s time while also making necessary
progress on my dissertation.

Thank you,
[Your name]
```

#### Strategy #4: Work in Parallel (When Possible)

**Don't Wait Completely**:

While waiting for feedback on Chapter 3:
- Start outlining Chapter 4
- Read additional literature
- Work on other dissertation sections (acknowledgments, appendices, references)
- Prepare for defense (practice talks)
- Work on publications

**Advice from Expert**:
> "While awaiting feedback on one chapter, begin drafting subsequent sections. **Plan for approximately one month between submission and receiving all committee feedback. This prevents delays from stalling your entire project.**"

**Limitations**:
- Can't finalize next chapter without feedback on previous
- Can't defend without all chapters reviewed
- Some chapters depend on previous chapters

**But**: Can maintain momentum and not waste months

#### Strategy #5: Use Alternative Feedback Sources

**While Waiting** (don't just sit idle):

**FREE Options**:
- **Writing center**: Get feedback on structure, organization, clarity
- **Peer writing group**: Reader perspective from fellow PhD students
- **Committee members**: "While waiting for Professor X, would you be willing to provide preliminary feedback on my methodology?"

**Paid Options** (Fast Turnaround!):
- **Scribbr**: 1-week turnaround on editing + structure check (~$317 for 10K words)
- **Scribbr 48-hour rush**: $349 (available!)
- **GradCoach**: Usually 1-2 weeks for content review (~$400-600)
- **Dissertation Coach**: Ongoing support, faster than most advisors

**AI Tools** (INSTANT):
- **Thesify**: Upload chapter, get immediate structured feedback
- **Paperpal**: Real-time feedback as you write
- **Cost**: $20-40/month vs. months of waiting

**Strategy**:
1. Submit to advisor
2. Immediately get AI feedback (same day)
3. Week 2: Schedule writing center appointment
4. Week 3: Get peer group feedback
5. Week 4: If still no advisor feedback, consider professional service
6. Week 6: Have external feedback to compare when advisor finally responds

**Benefits**:
- Keep making progress
- Multiple perspectives
- Have solid draft when advisor finally responds
- Might discover advisor feedback isn't even necessary (work is strong)

## Solutions for Preventing Slow Turnaround

### For Advisors: Meeting Turnaround Standards

#### Time Management Strategies

**Block Time for Feedback**:
- Schedule "dissertation feedback days" on calendar
- Protect this time like you would a class
- Batch student feedback sessions

**Triage System**:
- **Within 1 week**: Short pieces, pre-defense edits
- **Within 2-3 weeks**: Regular chapters
- **Within 4-6 weeks**: Full drafts, multiple chapters

**Prioritization**:
- Students near defense: Highest priority
- Students whose funding is ending: High priority
- Early-stage students: Can wait slightly longer (but communicate!)

#### Communication is Key

**Even if you can't meet timeline**:
```
Email Template for Advisors:

Subject: Chapter 3 - Delayed Timeline

Dear [Student],

Thank you for submitting Chapter 3. I wanted to let you know that I
received it and have it in my queue.

Unfortunately, I'm running behind due to [conference travel/grant
deadline/illness]. I won't be able to provide feedback by [original
target date].

I will have comments for you by [new date - be realistic!]. I apologize
for the delay.

In the meantime, feel free to [continue with Chapter 4/work on X/meet
with Committee Member Y for preliminary feedback].

Best,
Professor [Name]
```

**What this accomplishes**:
- Student knows you received it (reduces anxiety)
- Clear new timeline (reduces uncertainty)
- Shows respect for student's time
- Suggests productive use of waiting time

#### Delegation & Support

**For Longer Documents**:
- Ask postdocs or advanced PhD students to provide preliminary feedback
- Committee members can review specific sections
- Writing center can help with structure/clarity

**You focus on**:
- Research quality and methodology
- Field contribution
- High-level feedback

## Technology Solutions

### Tools for Faster Feedback Delivery

#### **Voice Comments**

**Kaizena** (Google Docs add-on):
- Leave audio comments instead of typing
- Faster to explain complex feedback verbally
- Students get tone/nuance

**Benefits for Advisors**:
- Can provide feedback while walking/commuting
- Faster than typing detailed comments
- More personal

#### **Track Changes & Comments** (Microsoft Word/Google Docs)

**Use efficiently**:
- **First pass**: Read through, mark major issues only
- **Second pass**: Detailed line-by-line (if needed)
- **Summary comment**: Prioritized list of revisions

**Time-saving**:
- Focus on high-level issues first
- Don't wordsmith every sentence (not your job)
- Use comment banks for common issues

#### **Video Feedback**

**Loom** or similar screen recording:
- Record yourself scrolling through document and commenting
- 20 minutes of video = lots of feedback
- Faster than writing everything out

### Automated Reminders

**For Students**:

**Set up email drafts in advance**:
- Day 1: "Thank you for submitting" auto-send
- Week 3: "Haven't heard back, following up" (review before sending)
- Week 5: "Still need feedback" (review before sending)

**Calendar reminders**:
- Week 3: "Follow up on Chapter 3"
- Week 5: "Escalate Chapter 3 if no response"
