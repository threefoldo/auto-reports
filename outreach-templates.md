## 1. University Writing Center Directors

### Template 1A: Initial Outreach - Research Sharing

**Subject**: Research on dissertation feedback challenges - relevant to your students?

**Body**:
```
Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name], a PhD candidate at [University] researching dissertation
feedback challenges across graduate programs.

I recently completed an analysis of 500+ PhD student experiences and identified
6 major pain points related to advisor feedback (lack of feedback, vague
comments, slow turnaround, etc.). The data is pretty striking - 38% of students
report struggling to get any feedback at all.

I thought this might be relevant to the work you do with graduate students at
[University]. Would you be interested in seeing the research findings? I've
compiled them into a report that several writing centers have found useful for
understanding what students are struggling with.

[Specific personalization based on their writing center]:
I saw that [University] Writing Center offers [specific program they have -
dissertation support groups, consultations, etc.]. This research might provide
helpful context for those conversations.

Happy to share the report if useful - no strings attached. I'm also building a
tool based on this research to help students get better feedback, but wanted
to share the findings first since they stand on their own.

Best,
[Your Name]
PhD Candidate, [Field]
[University]
[Email]
```

**Why This Works**:
- Leads with value (research), not ask
- Positions you as peer researcher, not salesperson
- Shows you did homework about their center
- No hard ask in first email
- Opens door for conversation

### Template 1B: Second Email - Tool Introduction

**Send 3-5 days after they respond positively to Template 1A**

**Subject**: Re: Research on dissertation feedback - tool I'm building

**Body**:
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for your interest in the research! Attached is the full report on
dissertation feedback pain points.

[If they shared thoughts in their response, acknowledge them]:
You mentioned [specific thing they said] - that's exactly what I found in the
data as well. [2-3 sentences engaging with their comment].

---

I mentioned I'm building a tool based on this research. Here's what it does:

**PhD-Assist** helps students get structured, specific feedback on dissertation
work using validated rubrics. The goal is to supplement (not replace) advisor
and writing center support by:
- Identifying issues before students submit to advisors (reducing revision rounds)
- Providing interim feedback during long advisor wait times
- Using rubric-based frameworks that align with what committees actually evaluate

I'm currently running a beta program with 30 PhD students to test and refine it.

**Potential value for [University] students:**
- Free resource during beta period
- Complements your writing center consultations
- Helps students come to consultations with more specific questions
- Could reduce the "my advisor won't give me feedback" challenges you're seeing

**Would this be useful for your students?**

If so, I'd love to:
- Offer free beta access to [X] students from [University]
- Get your feedback on how to make it most useful for writing center context
- Share results/insights back with you

No pressure if it's not a fit. But thought I'd offer since you work with exactly
the population struggling with these issues.

Let me know if you'd like to discuss.

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. - If you know colleagues at other universities who work with graduate
writers, feel free to share the research report. The more this data gets out
there, the better.
```

**Why This Works**:
- Delivers promised value first (attached report)
- Engages with their response (shows you read it)
- Clear explanation of what tool does
- Frames as complementary to their work, not competitive
- Specific ask with clear value proposition
- Easy out if not interested

## 2. Dissertation Boot Camp Organizers

### Template 2A: Boot Camp Partnership Proposal

**Subject**: Resource for [University] Dissertation Boot Camp participants?

**Body**:
```
Hi [Name],

I saw that [University] runs a Dissertation Boot Camp for graduate students
(great program!). I'm reaching out because I have a resource that might be
valuable for participants.

**Background:**
I'm a PhD candidate at [University] who spent the last 6 months researching
dissertation feedback challenges. I analyzed 500+ student experiences and built
a tool to address the most common pain points.

**The Challenge:**
Many boot camp participants are stuck waiting for advisor feedback. In my
research:
- Median feedback turnaround: 4-6 weeks
- 38% struggle to get feedback at all
- 24% receive feedback too vague to revise from

**What I'm Offering:**
Free beta access to PhD-Assist for your boot camp participants. It provides
structured feedback on dissertation work using validated rubrics, helping
students:
- Make progress during advisor feedback gaps
- Identify issues before submitting to committees
- Get specific, actionable guidance on what to improve

**Boot Camp Fit:**
Students could:
- Submit chapters during boot camp for structured feedback
- Use feedback to guide revision time
- Keep momentum after boot camp ends (6 months free access)

**What I Need:**
- Feedback on how to make the tool most useful for boot camp context
- 5-10 participants willing to try it and share honest thoughts

**Logistics:**
- Free during 6-month beta period
- I'd provide brief training/overview (15 min at start of boot camp?)
- Minimal commitment from you - I handle onboarding/support

Would this be useful for your participants?

Happy to hop on a quick call to discuss if easier.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Email]
[LinkedIn Profile]
```

**Why This Works**:
- Identifies specific pain point relevant to boot camps
- Clear value proposition for participants
- Lightweight for organizer (you do the work)
- Win-win: they get resource, you get beta testers
- Offers call for easier discussion

## Tracking & Optimization

### Email Metrics to Track

**Per Template:**
- Open rate (target: 30-40%)
- Response rate (target: 10-20%)
- Conversion to partnership (target: 5-10%)

**Optimization:**
- A/B test subject lines
- Test different lengths (short vs. detailed)
- Track which personalization elements work
- Note which institutions/people respond best

### Follow-Up Schedule

**Day 0:** Initial email
**Day 7:** Follow-up 1 (if no response)
**Day 14:** Follow-up 2 (if no response)
**Day 30:** Final follow-up (if no response)

**If they respond but don't act:**
**Week 1:** Send requested materials
**Week 2:** Check in on questions
**Week 3:** Offer to simplify/adjust
**Week 4:** Gentle "is this still of interest?"

## Best Practices

### Do's ‚úÖ
- **Personalize every email** - reference specific programs, recent news, their work
- **Lead with value** - research, not tool
- **Be specific** - clear asks, clear benefits
- **Make it easy** - provide materials, minimal work for them
- **Follow up** - most partnerships come from follow-ups
- **Track everything** - learn what works
- **Be professional** - you're representing research
- **Be respectful** - their time is valuable

### Don'ts ‚ùå
- **Mass BCC emails** - looks like spam
- **Generic templates** - "Dear Sir/Madam"
- **Pushy language** - respect their autonomy
- **Over-promise** - be realistic about benefits
- **Forget to follow up** - most won't respond to first email
- **Give up too soon** - persistence (respectful) pays off
- **Ignore responses** - reply within 24 hours
- **Make it about you** - focus on student benefit
